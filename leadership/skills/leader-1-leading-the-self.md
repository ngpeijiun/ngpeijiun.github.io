## Automatic Cycle of Behaviour

1.  Automaticity is a very important aspect about our behaviour. We are all in our work life and also personal lives, we are bombarded with event situations that we encounter. If we are not careful, we act in an automatic manner and that is the principle of automaticity in psychology in organisation behaviour.

2.  Let us imagine a situation at office. You are working in office on a project, and the boss comes to you and tells you that you are being moved out of a project. Now, when this situation occurs, then this event occurs in front of you, there are bound to be some thoughts that come to us automatically. Why did the boss ask me to be moved out of a project? Why am I being moved out of this particular project? Is there something wrong in my performance? Is there some problem with how I'm working?

    These thoughts give rise to certain emotions. I may feel sad, I may feel fearful, I may also feel anger. When we have experienced these thoughts and emotions, we are also then bound to take certain actions, which then lead to another set of events and situations that unfold and that's the whole idea of automaticity.

3.  Events: The situation event that occurs is the boss tells us that there is something happening and you are being moved out of the project, so that is an event. It is very important to understand that the events are beyond our control. We don't control these events. I don't control what the boss tells me.

    Thoughts and Emotion: But then, these events give rise to certain thoughts and emotions. I feel these emotions, maybe anger, maybe sad, maybe fear and then these are also accompanied with certain thoughts. What is the problem? Why am I being moved out of this project? Is there something wrong with me?

    Actions: Based on these thoughts, I act in a particular manner. I may feel sad, I may therefore act in a lethargic manner, I may not like to work. At home, also when I go back, I may not behave properly with children, spouse, or others in family.

    New Events: Now your actions. The actions that we are taking give rise to newer events. You act in an improper manner, that would give rise to a new set of events unfolding in front of you and who will manage it? Well, it is none other than us who will have to manage these events. This negative cycle can keep on continuing.

    Friends, this is what we call as the automatic cycle of behaviour.
