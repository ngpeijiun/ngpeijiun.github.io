## Introduction

1.  In an increasingly complex world, the practice of leadership is becoming more and more important for leaders and organisations today. In simpler words, leadership is the practice of influencing people to meet the challenges that will enable them to achieve important individual and organisational goals. In a world that is characterised by changes and turbulence, what could leaders do to improve their own art and craft as they lead organisations?

2.  We will explore topics such as mindfulness and emotional intelligence, which are essential for leading oneself. Additionally, we will discuss various leadership styles, authority, power, and influence, along with key aspects of organisational dynamics, including politics, negotiations, and supportive communication. Other important areas include decision-making biases, ethical dilemmas, organisational culture, and change management.

3.  One of the modules of the course would also talk about the great Indian epic, the Mahabharata and the lessons we can derive as leaders from the ethics. The course touches upon various disciplines such as philosophy, spirituality, business management, psycology, history, ethics, and it is an amalgamation of various concepts that we hope would be useful for you as leaders.

4.  This is a beginner course designed for professionals from diverse work backgrounds. The course helps you lean on your experiences and strengthen your capacity to lead across boundaries, from any political or organisational position, with or without authority. The course is built on the core philosophy that leaders are not always born, leaders can also be made. The aim of this course is to equip you with certain skills that we hope would be useful for you as carry on on your journey of leadership.
